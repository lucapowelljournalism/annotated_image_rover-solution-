From Harlem with Love

Yuri Kochiyama was a civil rights activist known for her association with Maoist beliefs and with Malcolm X, who at a protest in Harlem in 1963. She was an advocate for reparations for post WW2 Japanese-American internees, as well as for protesting the Vietnam war. She died in June of 2014.

The Black Lives Matter movement began in 2013, after the shooting of Trayvon Martin in Florida drew international attention to systemic racism towards black people.

Malcolm X was a minister and black nationalist. He first moved to Harlem in 1943. He would return in March of 1954 to lead Temple Number 7, a Sunni Islam mosque on 130th St. The temple was destroyed in 1965, after Malcolm X's assassination.

This quote from Malcolm X is actually a cut off version of the original, which reads “The only way we’ll get freedom for ourselves is to identify ourselves with every oppressed people in the world. We are blood brothers to the people of Brazil, Venezuelan Haiti and Cuba - yes, Cuba too."

Malcolm X was very interested in Cuba, and famously arranged to meet Fidel Castro at the Hotel Theresa in Harlem in 1959. Malcolm was later quoted as saying that Fidel Castro was "the only white person he ever liked."

On the corner of 125th Street and Old Broadway, community members of all ages have banded together to paint a mural remembering two prominent local activists: Malcolm X and Yuri Kochiyama. The project site, once home to the Black-owned soul food restaurant and nightclub “Concerto West” and now a Columbia-owned building, has become the canvas for From Harlem with Love: A Mural Project for Yuri & Malcolm by way of hundreds of community volunteers in their effort to preserve the culture of West Harlem activism.
